FCLE Resource Links Directory

Curated links to study materials, sample questions, and exam information for the Florida Civic Literacy Exam

Official FLDOE State Universities Florida Colleges Supplemental Tools Verified March 2026
About This Directory: All links were verified in March 2026. The FCLE is an 80-question, multiple-choice, computer-based exam administered by Cambium Assessments. A passing score is 48/80 (60%). Links are organized by source type: official state resources, State University System (SUS) institutions, Florida College System (FCS) institutions, and supplemental third-party tools. "Last Updated" dates reflect the most recent modification date reported by the source page or, where unavailable, the date of the most recent content change found during verification. Pages with no visible date are marked N/A.

Official Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) Resources

Primary authoritative sources — all exam content is based on these documents

Official
FLDOE — Florida Civic Literacy Exam (FCLE) Main Page
Last Updated: Continuously maintained | Publisher: Florida Dept. of Education
URL https://www.fldoe.org/accountability/assessments/k-12-student-assessment/fcle.stml
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ✅ Contains FCLE Sample Questions
The official FLDOE hub for the FCLE. Links to the Supplemental Guide PDF, Sample Items PDF, the online practice test, and statewide exam results. This is the definitive starting point for all FCLE preparation. Also contains the statute references (SB 1108 / s. 1003.4282) and exam structure details.
Official
FLDOE — Supplemental Guide for the FCLE (PDF)
Last Updated: March 31, 2022 | Publisher: FLDOE Office of Assessment
URL https://www.fldoe.org/core/fileparse.php/5663/urlt/SuppGuideFCLE.pdf
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ⚠️ Contains Sample Questions (limited)
Direct PDF download of the official FLDOE Supplemental Study Guide. Contains the four exam competency domains with detailed topic lists, hyperlinks to primary source documents (CPALMS, Oyez, Archives.gov), and guidance on what content areas appear on the exam. This is the single most important document for FCLE preparation.
Official
FLDOE — FCLE Sample Items (PDF)
Last Updated: 2022 | Publisher: FLDOE / Cambium Assessments
URL https://www.fldoe.org/core/fileparse.php/5663/urlt/FCLESampleItem.pdf
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ✅ Contains FCLE Sample Questions
Direct PDF of official FCLE sample test items with answer key. Produced by the Florida DOE/Cambium Assessments. Shows actual question format and style. Note: FLDOE states that sample questions are illustrative only and should not be used to predict performance or guide classroom instruction.
Official
FLDOE — FCLE Online Practice Test (Cambium TDS Portal)
Last Updated: Continuously maintained | Publisher: Cambium Assessments / FLDOE
URL https://login4.cambiumtds.com/student_core/V176/Pages/LoginShell.aspx?c=Florida_PT&a=Student
❌ Study Materials (testing platform only) ✅ Contains FCLE Sample Questions
The official Cambium testing platform where students can experience sample FCLE questions in the same interface used for the real exam. Log in as "Guest User" with "Guest Session" toggled ON. Select Grade Level "30," then "Sample Items," and choose "Start FCLE Sample Items." The sample test is unscored — see the Sample Items PDF for answer keys.
Official
CPALMS — Florida's Official Standards & Resources Platform
Last Updated: Continuously maintained | Publisher: Florida DOE / FSU
URL https://www.cpalms.org/public/search/Standard
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ❌ Does Not Contain FCLE Sample Questions
CPALMS is Florida's official toolbox for educational standards and resources, referenced extensively in the FLDOE Supplemental Guide. Includes civics, U.S. history, and American government standards aligned to the FCLE competencies. Use to find standards-aligned reading materials and lesson resources.

State University System (SUS) — FCLE Resources

Florida's 12 public universities — most provide exam information pages; some offer dedicated prep materials

University
University of Central Florida (UCF) — University Testing Center FCLE Page
Last Updated: N/A (current academic year) | Publisher: UCF Testing Center
URL https://utc.sswb.ucf.edu/fcle/
⚠️ Study Materials (links to FLDOE resources) ❌ Does Not Contain UCF Sample Questions
UCF's official FCLE testing page covers exam formats (80-question 2021 FCLE and legacy 100-question FCLT), scheduling procedures, fees ($30 per in-person attempt), and retake policies. Also details Canvas-based practice course access and alternative pathways (CLEP/AP). Non-UCF students may also test here with authorization.
University
Florida State University (FSU) — Academic Center for Excellence Civic Literacy Resources
Last Updated: 2025 | Publisher: FSU ACE
URL https://ace.fsu.edu/civic-literacy-resources
⚠️ Study Materials (links to FLDOE resources) ❌ Does Not Contain FSU Sample Questions
FSU's ACE civic literacy page explains the transition from the older online Citizenship/Naturalization Exam to the new in-person FCLE for Fall 2024+ students. Includes details on the transition timeline (deadline August 2025 for older exam option) and links to official preparation resources.
University
University of Florida (UF) — Civic Literacy Requirement (Catalog)
Last Updated: 2025–2026 Academic Catalog | Publisher: UF Academic Catalog
URL https://catalog.ufl.edu/UGRD/academic-regulations/civics_requirement/
⚠️ Requirement Overview (not prep materials) ❌ Does Not Contain Sample Questions
UF's official academic catalog entry for the Civic Literacy requirement. Explains cohort definitions (Cohorts 1–4), which exam versions satisfy which requirements, and how AP/CLEP credit interacts with the civic literacy mandate. Essential for understanding graduation requirements but not a study resource itself.
University
Florida International University (FIU) — Civic Literacy Competency Requirement

⚠️ Study Materials (links to FLDOE resources) ❌ Does Not Contain FIU Sample Questions
FIU provides one of the more detailed university FAQ pages on the civic literacy requirement — covering both the FCLT (100-question legacy) and FCLE (80-question current), cohort eligibility, remote vs. in-person testing, fees ($35), dual enrollment rules, and CLEP alternatives. Includes link to schedule the exam at Modesto Maidique or Biscayne Bay campuses.

Florida Atlantic University (FAU) — Civic Literacy Requirement & Free Canvas Prep Course

FAU stands out among SUS institutions for offering a free Canvas-based FCLE study course open to all FAU students. The prep page also references FAU's own FCLE prep videos on YouTube (FAU Civic Literacy Test Prep 1 & 2). One of the more developed university-level study resource offerings.

Supplemental & Third-Party Resources

Free tools, flashcard platforms, and open courses useful for FCLE preparation — not officially endorsed by FLDOE

Supplemental
FSCJ Open Textbook — Florida Civic Literacy Exam Study Guide (Pressbooks)
Last Updated: 2024 (CC-BY 4.0 License) | Publisher: FSCJ Library & Learning Commons
URL https://fscj.pressbooks.pub/fcle/
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ❌ Does Not Contain FCLE Sample Questions
A complete, freely downloadable FCLE study textbook published under Creative Commons. Edited by Shannon Dew, Lisa Taylor, and Carson Welch at FSCJ. This is one of the few purpose-built open-access textbooks designed specifically for the FCLE — available in web, PDF, and eBook formats. Any instructor may adapt or reuse under CC-BY 4.0.

Supplemental
Modern States — Free American Government Course (CLEP Prep)
Last Updated: Continuously maintained | Publisher: Modern States Education Alliance
URL https://modernstates.org/course/american-government/
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ⚠️ Contains Sample Questions (CLEP-style, not FCLE-specific)
Modern States offers a 100% free, self-paced 4-week online course (approximately 6 hours/week) covering American Government content that aligns with FCLE competencies. After completing the course, students can apply for a free CLEP exam voucher. Passing the CLEP American Government exam satisfies both the FCLE course and assessment requirements — making this a full alternative pathway to the exam itself. Referenced by virtually every Florida college guide.

Khan Academy's free AP U.S. Government and U.S. History courses cover nearly all FCLE content areas. Includes video lessons, articles, and AP-style practice questions. Particularly strong on the Constitution, branches of government, civil rights, and landmark Supreme Court cases. Recommended by multiple Florida college LibGuides as a core supplemental resource.

Supplemental
Oyez Project — Landmark Supreme Court Cases
Last Updated: Continuously maintained | Publisher: Illinois Tech Chicago-Kent College of Law
URL https://www.oyez.org/cases
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ❌ Does Not Contain FCLE Sample Questions
Oyez is directly hyperlinked in the FLDOE Supplemental Guide as the recommended source for landmark Supreme Court case summaries. Provides plain-language case overviews, oral argument audio, and decision summaries. Essential for FCLE Domain 4 (Landmark Impact on Law & Society). The FLDOE Supplemental Guide lists every case students should know and links directly to Oyez summaries.
Supplemental
National Archives — Founding Documents (Declaration, Constitution, Bill of Rights)
Last Updated: Continuously maintained | Publisher: U.S. National Archives and Records Administration
URL https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ❌ Does Not Contain FCLE Sample Questions
The National Archives hosts official transcripts of the Declaration of Independence, U.S. Constitution, Bill of Rights, and other foundational documents directly referenced on the FCLE. Also referenced in the FLDOE Supplemental Guide. Essential for accurate, authoritative text of primary source documents that appear in exam questions.
Supplemental
National Constitution Center — Interactive Constitution
Last Updated: Continuously maintained | Publisher: National Constitution Center
URL https://constitutioncenter.org/the-constitution
✅ Contains FCLE Study Materials ❌ Does Not Contain FCLE Sample Questions
The NCC's Interactive Constitution provides clause-by-clause analysis of every provision of the Constitution and all 27 amendments, written by leading constitutional scholars from across the political spectrum. Excellent for deep study of Domains 2 (U.S. Constitution) and 3 (Founding Documents). Also hosts the Declaration of Independence, Federalist Papers references, and a historic document library.
